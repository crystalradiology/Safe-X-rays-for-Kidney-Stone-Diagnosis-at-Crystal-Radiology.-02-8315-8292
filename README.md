Understanding Kidney Stones
Kidney stones are hard, crystal-like deposits that form when minerals and salts in urine become highly concentrated. Over time, these substances bind together, forming stones that can vary in size from tiny grains to large obstructions.
Common symptoms include:
•	Sharp pain in the lower back or side
•	Pain radiating to the groin
•	Blood in the urine
•	Nausea or vomiting
•	Pain or burning during urination
•	Frequent urge to urinate
Because symptoms can mimic other conditions, medical imaging plays a crucial role in confirming the diagnosis.
How Kidney Stones Were Diagnosed in the Past?
Traditionally, diagnosing kidney stones often required invasive imaging techniques such as intravenous urography or retrograde pyelography. These methods involved contrast injections and longer procedure times, which could be uncomfortable for patients and carried additional risks.
Advances in medical imaging have significantly improved patient experience. Today, X-rays to Detect Kidney Stones at Crystal Radiology offer a much simpler and more patient-friendly diagnostic pathway.
How X-ray Imaging Detects Kidney Stones
X-ray imaging uses controlled doses of electromagnetic radiation to create detailed images of the urinary tract, including the kidneys, ureters, and bladder. Most kidney stones—especially calcium-based stones—are clearly visible on X-ray images due to their density.
During the scan:
•	You lie comfortably on an imaging table
•	The procedure is quick and painless
•	No injections or contrast agents are usually required
•	Images are captured within minutes
This efficiency makes X-ray imaging an ideal first-line diagnostic tool for suspected kidney stones.

When Additional Imaging May Be Needed
While X-rays are highly effective, they may not detect all types of kidney stones, such as uric acid stones, which are less dense. In certain cases, your doctor may recommend complementary imaging to gain a more complete picture.
These may include:
•	Ultrasound, especially for pregnant patients
•	CT scans, for complex or unclear cases
•	Follow-up X-rays to monitor stone movement or treatment success
At Crystal Radiology, imaging decisions are tailored to each patient’s needs, ensuring diagnostic accuracy while prioritising safety.
